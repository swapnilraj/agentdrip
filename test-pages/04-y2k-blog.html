The Future of Web Design: Where Are We Headed?

I have been thinking a lot about where web design is going and I wanted to share some thoughts with the community. We are at an interesting crossroads right now.

Flash is getting more powerful every release. Macromedia just dropped Flash 5 with ActionScript and it is basically a programming language now. You can build entire applications in Flash. Some people are saying the future of the web is 100% Flash -- no HTML at all. Just one big SWF file.

But here is what I think people are missing: HTML itself is evolving. CSS is getting better. We are seeing the first real CSS layouts that do not use tables. The W3C is pushing XHTML. And there is this thing called "web standards" that a lot of smart people are advocating for.

My prediction: Flash will dominate for intros and interactive stuff, but the core web will stay HTML-based. The browsers will get better at rendering CSS. Tables for layout will eventually die. And we will look back at this era the way we look at the early days of any medium -- experimental, ugly, beautiful, and full of energy.

What do you all think? Where is web design going?

Respectfully disagree with FlashGuru. Flash sites are terrible for accessibility. Screen readers cannot read them. Search engines cannot index them. They take forever to load on 56k.

Have you guys seen A List Apart? Jeffrey Zeldman is doing amazing things with pure CSS. No tables, no Flash, just clean semantic HTML and stylesheets. THAT is the future.

Also: have you tried using a Flash site on a slow connection? That "loading... 47%" screen is not a good user experience lol.
