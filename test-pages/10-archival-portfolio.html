On the Structure of Vernacular Knowledge Systems

Nelson, T. · 1965 · ID: VKS-1965-001

Examines organizational patterns in informal knowledge archives, with attention to cross-referencing mechanisms that emerge without central coordination.1

Subjects: hypertext information architecture knowledge organization

Folksonomy and the Limits of Emergent Taxonomy

Vander Wal, T. · 2004 · ID: FLT-2004-017

An analysis of user-generated tagging systems and their divergence from controlled vocabularies in digital libraries.

Subjects: folksonomy taxonomy classification

Marginalia as Metadata: Annotation Practices in Private Libraries

Jackson, H.J. · 2001 · ID: MAM-2001-042

A survey of marginal annotation in printed books from the 16th to 20th century, treated as a precursor to modern tagging and commenting systems.2

Subjects: annotation book history metadata

The Card Catalog and Its Discontents

Krajewski, M. · 2011 · ID: CCD-2011-008

Traces the development of the library card catalog from Leibniz to digital databases, arguing that the card was the first general-purpose data processing technology.

Subjects: library science media archaeology data processing

As We May Think: Revisited

Bush, V. (commentary by Nyce, J.) · 1991 · ID: AWMT-1991-003

Reprint and critical commentary on Bush's 1945 essay proposing the memex, with analysis of its influence on hypertext and the World Wide Web.
